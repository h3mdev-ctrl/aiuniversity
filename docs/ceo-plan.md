## Vision

### North star
The pack is a **teacher**: it gives you the skill, ensures you can use it, then lets you go do your
own thing. Rails are training wheels, not a cage. teach → verify → release. Success = the recipient
gets the right outcome from the pack, on their own machine, without days of back-and-forth.

### 10x check
The corpus of encoded known-good paths is the moat. The bottleneck (and existential risk) is content
velocity: hand-authoring rots and can't keep pace with tool churn. The 10x unlock is
**authoring-by-demonstration** — the author performs a setup once and the pack generates itself —
which makes the corpus survivable across churn. Deferred to v2 (must prove the runner first).

## CEO review findings (rigor pass on held scope)
1. Security: a pack is arbitrary command execution on the recipient's machine. Safe for friends;
   gate before marketplace. (→ design doc "Security & trust surface".)
2. Remediation escape hatch: fix-fails → STOP + "human needed at step N", never loop/improvise.
   (→ design doc "core idea".)
3. Platform risk: Anthropic may absorb install-verification natively. The runner is a commodity; the
   moat is the curated corpus + Windows-first + trust. Build accordingly.
